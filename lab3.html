Sakeena Younus Lab #3 - Lesson 4

My Baking Journey

I have been baking for my whole life. It started out when I was a little girl, helping my mom mash the bananas
for banana bread and add the chocolate chips for cookies. As I got older, I started try new things on my own,
so it moved on to different flavored cookies, cupcakes, and my first not so pretty cakes. At the end of 2020,
I decided I wanted to make a birthday cake for my brother's birthday in January. For the entire month of December,
I researched all the tools and ingredients I would need, watched endless tutorials, and planned every step I would
take. The actual process of make the cake was very long, but also rewarding. It ended up turning out really well,
so I made the goal to make all of my family member's cakes for the year of 2020.

What I Learned

In the year 2020, I ended up making 7 different cakes. During each process I experimented with different flavors
and techniques, and found myself getting more confident in my skills. Each time I made a cake, I would estimate the
amount of time it took me to prepare all the components, as well as the decoration process, and I found that for
each cake, the time I spent would decrease. In January 2022, I experienced a full circle moment when I baked the same
brother another birthday cake. It was a complete one year journey, and when I look back, I see how much I have progressed,
not just in baking, but also with the willingness to take risks and try new things!

What the Future Holds

During 2021, I was baking a new cake about every two months, and I found myself really enjoying it and wanting to try
new things! I considered opening a business, and even created a baking Instagram account called Sweets By Sakeena.
After a lot of deliberation, I realized that creating a small may not be the best idea since it would take a lot of time
and effort, and I was very focused on school. I still really like the idea of one day creating a business, but for the
time being, I just bake for my friends and family whenever I can!

What I Made:

Through my year of baking, I was able to bake 10 different desserts consisting of cakes, cupcakes, and cheesecakes.
The different flavors included chocolate, vanilla, strawberry, red velvet, mint chocolate chip, and cookies and cream.
I made desserts for birthdays, anniversaries, and just for fun. I found that my ability to customize desserts, even
within similar flavors, increased. Customization with cakes is really easy because there are so many different elements
to creating cakes. You can change out the sponge flavor, fillings, frostings, decorations, and toppings, all to make
the cake perfect for the people or person you are making it for.

Goals

One thing that helped a immensely when continuously baking throughout the year was having a list of desserts and flavors
I wanted to test out. This allowed me to feel like I was doing something for myself, learning more, and achieving goals in
the process of creating desserts for others. A lot of the time, the flavors and designs of the cake were totally up to me,
so I was able to choose different techniques in creating the cakes that I wanted to try out. Some of these techniques
included drip cakes, watercolor cakes, several fillings and frostings, and different cake sizings for emphasis on the
design and decoration elements.
